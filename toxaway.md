# Toxaway Company / Lake Toxaway — E.H. Jennings Holdings Summary

Compiled 23 July 2026 from primary court reports, NRHP nomination, contemporary financial press, and cross-checked secondary local-history sources (Historic Toxaway Foundation, Transylvania County Library, etc.). Only facts with source support are stated; narrative color is labeled where secondary.

## Chronology

| Year | Event | Status |
|------|-------|--------|
| 1895–1896 | Toxaway Company formed by Pittsburgh group (C.H. Stolzenbach, W.D. Sharpe, G.W. Eisenbeis, J.F. Hays/J. Francis Hayes, C.W.R. Radeker and others). Purpose: land, timber, minerals in Sapphire Valley (Transylvania & Jackson Counties, NC). | Confirmed (incorporation references) |
| ~1890s–1902 | E.H. Jennings becomes largest shareholder. | Confirmed |
| 1 Jun 1902 | Dam construction begins (contractors Oates & Cowan, Asheville). | Confirmed |
| 8 Mar 1903 | Water first over spillway. | Confirmed |
| mid-Jul 1903 | Earthen dam completed (~500 ft long, 60 ft high). Lake Toxaway filled (first artificial lake in the Appalachians; ~3 mi long, 1 mi wide, ~14–15 mi shoreline, ~640 acres, ~5.3 billion gallons). | Confirmed |
| 1903 | Toxaway Inn (5-story, ~250 rooms) opens. Other properties under company control: Fairfield Inn, Sapphire Inn & Cottages, Lodge at Mt. Toxaway, Franklin Hotel (Brevard). | Confirmed |
| ~1911 | Toxaway Company enters receivership (reported bond debt ~$272,000). | Confirmed |
| 1911–1912 | E.H. Jennings (largest shareholder) and associates purchase the company’s holdings out of receivership for ~$100,000 (NRHP figure). Jennings becomes outright owner of dam, lake, inns, and ~27,910+ acres. | Confirmed (Primary + NRHP) |
| 1912–1916 | Jennings sells limited lakeside “cottage colony” lots (at least 16 parcels recorded) with strict residential-only deed restrictions. | Confirmed (Secondary indexes + local history) |
| 13 Aug 1916 | Dam fails ~7:00–7:10 p.m. after series of hurricanes / extreme rainfall (>20–24 in. in 24 hrs). ~5.3–5.4 billion gallons released; 30-ft wall of water into South Carolina. Only confirmed casualty: one blind mule. Extensive downstream damage. Toxaway Inn closes. | Confirmed |
| 1917 | Jennings sells ~4,500 acres to Jerome Moltz for logging. | Confirmed (Secondary) |
| 1916–1921 | Multiple damage suits filed. Principal case: *Commissioners of Pickens County and Oconee County, SC v. E.H. Jennings*, 181 N.C. 393, 107 S.E. 312 (N.C. Sup. Ct., filed 18 May 1921). Jennings held liable as owner. Related: *Smathers v. Jennings* (1914), *Fisher v. Toxaway Co.* (1916), Colonial Trust Co. v. Toxaway Company (index). | Confirmed (Primary for 1921 case) |
| 1916–1955 | Property remains under Jennings family control. Caretaker Tolvin T. Miller maintained the closed inn and remaining acreage for years. No dam rebuild. | Confirmed (Secondary) |
| 1923 onward | Estate of E.H. Jennings (executors include R.G. Jennings) continues to hold and convey former Toxaway lands (multiple deeds into the 1930s). | Confirmed (Transylvania County indexes) |
| 1955 | ~9,000 acres sold by Jennings family to David & Bertha Cosby. | Confirmed (Secondary) |
| 1960–1961 | Core property purchased by Lake Toxaway Estates, Inc. (later Lake Toxaway Company; R.D. Heinitsh Sr. et al.). Dam rebuilt; lake refilled. Modern private community established. | Confirmed (Secondary / modern company history) |
