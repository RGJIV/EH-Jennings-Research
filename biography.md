# Biography of Edward Henry Jennings (E. H. Jennings, Sr.)

Edward Henry Jennings (E. H. Jennings, Sr.) was a Pittsburgh-based industrialist, born August 10, 1852, in Bradys Bend, PA (Find a Grave ID 90963809). Son of Richard Morley Jennings Sr. (1819–1891) and Katharine Evans (1831–1907). He built a business empire in oil, banking, and real estate, with major ties to Pennsylvania (Pittsburgh, Allegheny/Westmoreland/Armstrong Counties) and North Carolina (Transylvania County for Toxaway). Active from the 1870s; died November 17, 1923, of pneumonia in Pittsburgh (obituary *Pittsburgh Gazette Times* Nov 18, 1923; funeral Nov 19; buried Homewood Cemetery, Section 14, Lot 84). Post-1911 owner of the Toxaway properties after $100,000 purchase out of receivership.

## Business Roles and Ventures (Verified Status)

- 1872–1891: Petroleum production with father.
- 1891 onward: Member of E. H. Jennings & Brothers (leading PA oil producers; confirmed).
- 1893: Founder and president of Columbia National Bank of Pittsburgh ("the oil bank"); confirmed in contemporary bank histories and 1910 newspapers; still identified as organizer at the 1927 merger with Bank of Pittsburgh, N.A.
- President, Colonial Trust Co. (confirmed in *Banks and Bankers of the Keystone State*).
- Major stockholder in the original Pure Oil Company (1895 independent, NJ-incorporated, Pittsburgh-area); sold large block of stock (~941k shares) in 1916–17 as part of the transaction that later produced the national Pure Oil Co. (son E.H. Jr. later served as VP of Pure Oil).
- Toxaway Company: stockholder, director, former vice president; purchased the company's holdings (including the dam) in 1911 for ~$100,000 (NRHP nomination confirmed).
- Federal Oil Co. and Transylvania Oil Syndicate: named only as family interests in the 1931 obituary of E.H. Jennings Jr. (no independent operational records yet retrieved).
- Royal Gas Company: claimed in earlier iterations as 1891 co-founding; **no independent confirmation retrieved as of 2026-07-23** — treat as unconfirmed.
- Other confirmed associations from contemporary sources: Delmar Oil Co. (treasurer/director), Jennings Oil Co. (president/director), Kanawha Oil Co. (treasurer/director), Manufacturers Light and Heat Co. (director), Dollar Savings Bank (trustee), Emerald Coal and Coke Co. (director), Citizen Savings Bank (director), Jennings Mines Co. (president/director).

## Key Events and Legal
- 1910: Arrested in Pittsburgh graft scandal (conspiracy/bribery for depositories; bail $10,000–$15,000; Spokane Press Jan 22, 1910); sentencing postponed with A.C. Griffin due to family illness (*Palestine Daily Herald* May 14, 1910).
- 1916: Toxaway Dam failure (Aug 13).
- 1921: *Commissioners of Pickens County and Oconee County, SC v. E.H. Jennings*, 181 N.C. 393, 107 S.E. 312 (N.C. Supreme Court, filed 18 May 1921). Jennings liable as owner after 1911 purchase; case fully verified from published reports.
- Other: Smathers v. Jennings (1914, Jackson County Journal).
