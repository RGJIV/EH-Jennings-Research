# Timeline of Edward Henry Jennings (All Events From Iterations and Repo)

Focus: Pre-1930 events only. Status notes added 2026-07-23 where claims were clarified or remain open.

- 1819: Father Richard Morley Jennings Sr. birth.
- 1831: Mother Katharine Evans birth.
- 1852: Born in Bradys Bend, PA (Find a Grave).
- 1855: First spouse Mary Jane Colwell birth.
- 1857: Sister Mary Katherine birth.
- 1861: Brother Richard Morley Jr. birth.
- 1864: Brother John Gundry birth.
- 1867: Sibling Jenifer Gundry birth/death.
- 1872–1891: Petroleum with father (early oil career).
- 1879: Married Mary Jane Colwell.
- 1880: Son Richard Gundry birth.
- 1887: Daughter Mary Brooks birth.
- 1891: Father death; member of E. H. Jennings & Brothers; McDonald oil field. (Royal Gas Company co-founding claim remains **unconfirmed**.)
- 1892: McDonald oil field development (*Pittsburg Dispatch* Dec 31, 1892) — Confirmed.
- 1893: Founded Columbia National Bank of Pittsburgh and became its president — Confirmed.
- 1896: First spouse death.
- 1898 (Post-Oct 3): WV oil leases (Tyler Co.; full list in biography; *Sistersville Oil Review* Oct 19, 1898) — Confirmed.
- 1900-1906: NC real estate mentions.
- 1903: Son Richard Gundry Yale graduation.
- **1904–05: E.H. Jennings & Brothers active in Sugar Grove / Knoxville oil field, Jefferson County, Ohio** (leases subject of *Williams v. Jennings* suit; well No. 8 on M.M. Culp farm reached Berea grit, Apr 1905). Partners named as Edward H., R.M., John G., and Everett. R.M. and John G. align with known brothers; Everett still unconfirmed. — Confirmed (primary newspaper).
- 1905: Remarried Ella Bailey; NC lumber entry (Buncombe County timber).
- 1905–1906: PA leases (Zelienople).
- 1907: Mother death; brother Richard Morley Jr. death.
- 1910: PA holdings (Venango); arrested in Pittsburgh graft scandal (bail $10,000–$15,000; president of Columbia National Bank, linked to Pure Oil / Colonial Trust / Jennings Bros.; *Spokane Press* Jan 22, 1910); sentencing postponed (*Palestine Daily Herald* May 14, 1910) — Confirmed.
- 1911: Purchased Toxaway properties ($100,000; NRHP Nomination) — Confirmed.
- 1912: Brother John Gundry death.
- 1914–1915: NC lawsuits (Smathers vs. Jennings; *Jackson County Journal* Apr 17, 1914) — Confirmed.
- 1916: Dam failure (Aug 13).
- 1916–17: Large Pure Oil stock sale (~941k shares) by E.H. Jennings (*Queen v. Jennings* 1919) — Confirmed.
- 1921: *Commissioners of Pickens & Oconee Counties, SC v. E.H. Jennings*, 181 N.C. 393 (N.C. Sup. Ct., filed 18 May 1921) — now Confirmed primary.
- 1923: Died of pneumonia; funeral Nov 19 (*Pittsburgh Gazette Times* Nov 18, 1923).
- 1925 / 1928: PA refining and gas partnership newspaper pages remain **Unconfirmed** (no E.H. text located on the cited pages).
- 1931: Son Edward Jr. death (Sep 3; *Evening Star* Sep 4, 1931 notes family Pure Oil / Federal Oil / Transylvania Oil Syndicate interests).
